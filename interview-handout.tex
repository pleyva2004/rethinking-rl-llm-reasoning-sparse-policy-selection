\section*{One-Liner}
RL post-training for math reasoning reranks $\sim$2\% of tokens at high-entropy decision points toward alternatives the base model already had in its top-5; an offline contrastive fine-tune gated on base entropy matches full RL at $\sim 1000\times$ less compute.

\section*{What's Novel}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Token-level mechanistic claim with a causal probe: oracle correction at reranked positions recovers RL's pass@1 exactly; random controls do not.
  \item Teacher-free locator: base entropy alone ($\tau \approx 1.4$ nats) identifies the intervention sites without ever consulting the RL model.
  \item Constructive alternative: \emph{ReasonMaxxer} drops the online RL loop, keeps GRPO's per-group advantage normalisation, applies CE only at decision points, KL-anchors elsewhere.
  \item RL's parametric delta is rank-32 LoRA on QKVO (0.27--0.49\% of params); rank-8 on $W_O$ alone nearly suffices.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Math in 60 Seconds}
Token-level predictive entropy under the base model
\[
  H_t = -\sum_{v} \pi_{\text{base}}(v \mid q, o_{<t}) \log \pi_{\text{base}}(v \mid q, o_{<t}).
\]
Decision-point set: $D = \{t : H_t > \tau\}$. Per-rollout advantage (from GRPO):
\[
  A_i = \frac{r_i - \bar r}{\sigma_r + \epsilon}, \quad r_i \in \{0,1\}.
\]
ReasonMaxxer loss:
\[
  \mathcal{L} = -\sum_i A_i \!\!\sum_{t \in D^{(i)}}\!\! \log \pi_\theta\big(o_t^{(i)} \,\big|\, q, o_{<t}^{(i)}\big) \;+\; \lambda \!\!\sum_{t \notin D^{(i)}}\!\! \mathrm{KL}\big( \pi_{\text{base}} \,\big\|\, \pi_\theta \big).
\]
Empirical: reranked fraction 1--4\%; the RL-promoted token sits at mean rank $\approx 2.2$ under the base; entropy at reranked positions is $5$--$12\times$ that at unchanged ones.

\section*{What I'd Push Back On}
\begin{itemize}
  \item ``Within base top-5'' is measured on already RL-trained checkpoints; partial circularity until verified on a held-out, truly untouched base.
  \item Threshold $\tau$ is tuned per setup. ``No RL needed'' partially trades the optimisation loop for a scalar grid-search.
  \item All wins are on math with verifiable scalar rewards. Open-ended tasks, agentic tool use, or tasks where the right intervention is \emph{low}-entropy fall outside the licensed scope --- and the framework predicts its own failure there.
  \item ``1000$\times$ cheaper'' compares to full RL training only; ReasonMaxxer still needs labeled-correctness rollouts. Accounting could be sharper.
\end{itemize}

\section*{My One Question}
\emph{Is the mean-rank-$\approx 2$ regularity an artifact of bounded RL learning rates inducing a Lipschitz constraint on rank displacement, or is there something deeper about why RL's correction never reaches past the second-most-likely base candidate?}
